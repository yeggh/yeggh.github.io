About Hamidreza

I am a Bachelors student at Sharif Unviersity of technology Computer Science & Engineering Department and research assistant in both Aalto university and MaxPlanck institute of Informatics.

My topics of interest involve optimization and computational learning theory as well as deep-learning and applications of machine learning especially in bio-informatics.

Research Experience

I am currently researching over two topics:

  1. Semi-Definite Programming using a natural dynamic known as the Slime Mold. In that, we have proposed a new algorithm inspired from a natural dynamic which is able to solve positive linear programs. I have started my research on this from 2020 under the supervision of Andreas Karrenbauer and published a paper here. The topic involves advanced linear algebra as well as a good understanding of dynamical system and convex optimization.

  2. RNA inverse folding using Graph Neural Networks. In that, we try to design an RNA sequence which folds into a certain given secondary structrure. The problem of RNA sequence design is one of primary importance in bio-engineering and we have tried to achieve this via a data-driven approach. I have started this research recently as a summer intern under the supervision of Pekka Orponen. The topic involves a baseline faimilarity of RNA structures and in-depth understanding of Neural Network architectures and machine learning in general.

You may find more detailed information about my research here.
